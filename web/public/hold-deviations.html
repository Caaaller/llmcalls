Root cause — USPS

gpt-4o-mini misinterprets "Please stay on the line at the end of this call to take a short survey" as hold queue phrasing.

The prompt tells the AI to set holdDetected: true for phrases like "please hold" and "stay on the line." The AI sees "stay on the line" and flags it — even though the context is a survey announcement, not hold music.

Fix options:

1. Tighten the prompt: "stay on the line" alone is NOT hold — only phrases like "please hold", "all agents are busy", "estimated wait time".

2. Require a corroborating signal (no IVR menu presented after the phrase, or wait-time mention).

Root cause — Best Buy

A 33-second gap in Deepgram transcription (while Best Buy's IVR was processing) tripped the backend silent-hold timer.

The silent-hold timer is a backend safety net: if no speech is transcribed for 30s, assume we're on hold (since hold music often doesn't transcribe). It resets on every IVR utterance AND every AI action.

In Best Buy's case: after the AI asked clarification at +28s, the IVR started replying ("I understand you want to...") then went silent for 33s while buffering its next response. The timer fired during that gap.

Fix options:

1. Bump timeout to 45-60s (user previously declined this).

2. Require two consecutive silent windows before firing (single gap = IVR thinking, repeated = real hold).

3. Don't fire silent hold if we recently asked a confirmation question — human response may take time.

Summary

USPS: AI hallucinated hold on the phrase "stay on the line" (meant for end-of-call survey, not hold queue). Pure prompt/AI issue.

Best Buy: Backend silent-hold timer fired during a 33s Deepgram gap caused by IVR buffering. The timer resets on AI/IVR activity but can't distinguish "IVR is thinking" from "we're on hold with music."

Both got terminated by the test framework's 15s post-hold exit — but the hold events themselves were generated by different mechanisms (AI judgment vs backend timer).
